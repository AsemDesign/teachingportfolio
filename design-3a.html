Design 3A — Design for the Digital Realm

Rutgers University – Mason Gross School of Arts
Teaching Assistant
Spring 2025

This course trains students to research, analyze, prototype, and develop design concepts across three scales of digital media: projection, web browser, and mobile apps. Students explore how the unique features of each medium shape social interactions, considering contexts such as karaoke, subculture communication, and civic engagement. The course emphasizes experimentation with UI/UX design while maintaining focus on audience, context, and conceptual goals.

Students develop fluency across multiple media and techniques, gaining proficiency in motion graphics, web development, and interface design tools. The course is not primarily technical; rather, technical skills are learned as tools to support conceptual exploration and to expand the possibilities of media-driven design practice. Class time combines lectures, studio work, and critiques, with additional individual research, readings, and project development outside of class.

Learning Goals

By the end of the course, students will:

  • Research, analyze, and develop design concepts for multiple digital media formats
  • Apply UI/UX design principles to projects across projection, web, and mobile interfaces
  • Consider audience, context, and medium-specific characteristics in design decisions
  • Experiment with motion graphics, web development, and interface design tools
  • Develop conceptual and technical fluency across multiple media and scales
  • Explore how digital formats shape social interaction and communication
  • Engage in iterative design processes with in-class exercises and workshops
  • Critically assess their own work and the work of peers in a design context
