Design 2A — Systems of Design

Rutgers University – Mason Gross School of Arts
Adjunct Professor
Fall 2025

Design 2A: Visual Systems and Identity introduces students to the structures designers use to create meaningful, functional, and visually compelling brands and identities. Building on skills developed in Visual Thinking, Design 1A, and Design 1B, students work across scales and mediums to let concepts drive the forms their work embodies. Through projects such as information campaigns, visual identities, books, résumés, and portfolios, students explore visual systems—sets of components and rules that communicate information clearly and consistently. The course emphasizes experimentation, risk-taking, and iterative development while fostering both conceptual thinking and technical skill. Students engage with critiques, lectures, readings, and demonstrations to build their craft, refine their personal design approach, and learn to articulate design decisions.

Learning Goals

By the end of the course, students will:

  • Understand visual systems as structured frameworks for communication
  • Develop conceptual and technical skills to design functional and aesthetic solutions
  • Apply components and rules to create coherent multi-part design systems
  • Work across various scales and mediums to let concepts guide form
  • Generate and refine visual material through iterative experimentation
  • Develop attention to craft and visual problem-solving
  • Critically analyze and discuss both their own work and that of peers
  • Produce professional work, including information campaigns, visual identities, books, résumés, and portfolios
