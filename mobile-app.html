Mobile App Design

Rutgers Summer Art & Design Institute
Summer 2025
Teaching Instructor

This half-day Foundation course introduces students in grades 6–12 to designing and prototyping mobile applications using Figma. The course begins with an overview of User Experience (UX) and User Interface (UI) principles, then guides students through hands-on exercises including sketches, moodboards, and digital prototypes. Students analyze existing mobile apps, explore interactive elements, and refine their work through an iterative studio process, culminating in a gallery-style presentation where they showcase their final app prototypes and reflect on their design process.

Learning Goals

By the end of the course, students will:

  • Understand basic principles of UX and UI design
  • Navigate and use Figma to design mobile applications
  • Create sketches, collages, and moodboards to develop concepts
  • Design, prototype, and present functional mobile app interfaces
  • Apply typography, color palettes, and interactive elements
  • Experiment with animation and prototyping for user interaction
  • Reflect on their process and respond to constructive feedback
  • Communicate design ideas visually and verbally

Outcomes

Students will complete:

  • Daily exercises analyzing and prototyping mobile apps
  • Hand sketches, paper collages, and digital prototypes in Figma
  • A fully realized mobile app prototype following UX/UI principles
  • A final gallery presentation with peer and instructor feedback
