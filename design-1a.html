Design 1A — Type and Typography

Rutgers University – Mason Gross School of Arts
Adjunct Professor
Summer 2025

Type and Typography explores how written language becomes visual design. Students engage with the fundamentals of type—including fonts, spacing, grids, color, and composition—through hands-on studio projects. The course emphasizes both conceptual thinking and technical skill, encouraging experimentation, iteration, and dialogue with historical and contemporary typographic references. Work produced spans both print and digital formats, allowing students to develop fluency with modern design tools while cultivating a distinct visual voice.

Learning Goals

By the end of the course, students will:

  • Understand typography as a tool of communication and expression
  • Learn key typographic terminology
  • Work with type at both small and large scales
  • Become well-acquainted with a robust list of key typefaces
  • Become familiar with historical and contemporary designers
  • Understand how graphic design fits into the broader design discourse
  • Demonstrate an understanding of graphic principles and techniques
  • Set and manipulate type in Adobe InDesign, Illustrator, and After Effects
  • Differentiate between different typographic conventions across print and screen-based platforms
